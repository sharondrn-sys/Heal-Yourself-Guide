Daily Mineral Schedule

Exact timing for every supplement in this protocol — morning through evening. The timing is not arbitrary. It is built around the absorption conflicts between minerals, meal spacing requirements, and the energising vs. calming effects of different forms. Follow this and the interactions take care of themselves.

⚠️ Medical Disclaimer — Read Before Starting

This schedule is for educational purposes only. It is not medical advice and does not replace the guidance of a qualified healthcare practitioner. The author is not a doctor. The minerals in this protocol — iodine, copper, zinc, and magnesium — are potent and interact with each other and with medications. Incorrect doses or forms can cause harm. Do not exceed the suggested amounts. If you have a thyroid condition, kidney disease, or are on prescription medication, consult a practitioner before starting. Pregnant or breastfeeding: do not use high-dose iodine without medical supervision. Start with the minimal version of this schedule and increase gradually.

The Rules That Govern This Schedule

Before you look at the timing, understand why it's structured this way. These are the non-negotiable interaction rules:

⚠️
Zinc and Copper NEVER Together
They share the same intestinal absorption pathway. Taking them simultaneously means the zinc blocks the copper. Minimum gap: 2 hours. The schedule puts zinc at breakfast, copper at dinner.
⚠️
Iodine Always With Selenium
Therapeutic iodine without selenium stresses thyroid tissue. They go together, every time, no exceptions. Both taken with breakfast.
⚠️
Baking Soda NOT With Meals
Neutralises stomach acid needed for digestion and B12 absorption. Always 30 minutes before food or 2 hours after. Morning baking soda is taken before breakfast.
🌙
Magnesium Glycinate at Night
Glycinate is calming and mildly sedating — intentionally. Take 1 hour before bed. Use magnesium malate in the morning if you also need daytime magnesium support.
☀️
Iodine in the Morning Only
Iodine is energising. Taking it after 2pm can disrupt sleep in sensitive people. Always with breakfast, never at night.
🍽️
Zinc With Food, Not Empty Stomach
Zinc on an empty stomach causes nausea reliably. Always take with breakfast or a small snack. Never swallow zinc first thing in the morning on nothing.
⚠️ Start Slowly — One New Mineral Every 1–2 Weeks
Do not start this entire schedule on day one. Begin with magnesium glycinate at night only — for one full week. Then add iodine + selenium. Then zinc. Then copper. This sequencing lets you identify any reactions and keeps the interaction effects manageable.

7:00 – 8:00 AM
Breakfast — With Food
All morning supplements taken with or just after a meal
⚪
Zinc Bisglycinate
15–25mg · With food · Never on empty stomach
Take mid-meal or just after eating. Zinc on an empty stomach causes reliable nausea. Morning is the correct time — copper goes in the evening, minimum 2 hours after this zinc dose. Do not take with copper.
Must-Have
🧿
Iodine (Lugol's 5% or Nascent)
Lugol's: 1–2 drops in water · Nascent: 1–3 drops
Morning only — iodine is energising and can disrupt sleep if taken after 2pm. Take in a small glass of water alongside or just after breakfast. Build dose gradually over 4–8 weeks. Never take without selenium.
Must-Have
🌊
Selenium 200mcg
200mcg selenomethionine · Same time as iodine
Always paired with iodine — they go together every single time. Selenium protects thyroid tissue during iodine metabolism and enables T4→T3 conversion. Do not take iodine without this.
Must-Have with Iodine
🏋️
Magnesium Malate
300–400mg elemental magnesium · With breakfast
The energising magnesium form — supports ATP production and Krebs cycle without the calming/sedating effect of glycinate. Use this in the morning only if you also have fatigue, fibromyalgia, or chronic low energy. If you only need sleep and anxiety support, glycinate at night is sufficient.
Optional — Fatigue/CFS
Breakfast Mineral Stack at a Glance
Zinc · Iodine · Selenium — these three always together at breakfast. If you're taking magnesium malate, add it here too. That's the full morning mineral stack. Everything else — copper, magnesium glycinate — is held for evening.

Afternoon Alkaline Drink
½ tsp baking soda + pinch cream of tartar + 250ml water
The potassium bitartrate (cream of tartar) adds potassium bicarbonate alongside sodium bicarbonate — a fuller electrolyte alkalising effect. Take at least 2 hours after breakfast, at least 2 hours before dinner. Not required — the morning drink alone provides significant benefit.

Transdermal magnesium bypasses the gut and absorbs directly into muscle tissue. Excellent for restless legs, cramps, and muscle soreness. Can be used at any time of day — morning or afternoon. A foot bath with magnesium flakes and sun-charged water is the most relaxing version.

6:00 – 7:30 PM
Dinner — With the Evening Meal
The copper window — at least 8 hours after morning zinc
🟤
Copper Bisglycinate
1–2mg · With dinner · Separated from zinc by at least 8 hours
This is the most important timing rule on this schedule. Zinc was taken at breakfast. Copper is taken at dinner. They must not overlap. If you took zinc at 7am, dinner at 6pm is an 11-hour gap — perfect. If you eat early and also took zinc late — double check the gap is at least 2 hours.
Must-Have (if supplementing zinc)
🦪
Oysters — Food Alternative
1–2 canned oysters with dinner · 2–3× per week
If you prefer food over supplements for copper: canned oysters at dinner provide naturally pre-balanced zinc and copper at approximately a 7:1 ratio. On oyster days, you may reduce or skip the copper bisglycinate. Pumpkin seeds are an alternative for those who dislike shellfish.
Optional — Food First Alternative
9:00 – 10:00 PM
Evening — Wind-Down
1 hour before sleep
🧠
Magnesium Glycinate
300–400mg elemental magnesium · 1 hour before bed
This is the single most important supplement on the entire schedule — and the one to start with if you're doing nothing else. Glycinate is calming, crosses the blood-brain barrier gently, relaxes muscles, and improves sleep architecture. It is working while you sleep, replenishing the magnesium debt that accumulates daily from stress, coffee, and processing.
Must-Have — Start Here
🔬
Magnesium L-Threonate
One serving (as directed) · Can combine with glycinate
If neurological decline, cognitive impairment, anxiety, or depression are primary concerns — add L-threonate at night alongside glycinate. This is the only form that meaningfully increases brain magnesium. It is more expensive and not essential for general mineral restoration — prioritise it for brain-specific applications.

As directed · Once levels are restored
Once you have completed 3–4 months of active iodine supplementation and feel stable, switch from Lugol's to kelp capsules for long-term maintenance. Lower dose, gentler on the system, provides trace co-factors alongside iodine. Selenium continues with this transition.

Evening — 3× per week
Bath Protocols
Transdermal mineral absorption + detox + alkalising

These are not luxuries. Transdermal absorption bypasses the gut entirely — this is how magnesium sulfate (Epsom salt) and magnesium chloride reach muscle tissue when gut absorption is compromised. The baking soda addition alkalises the skin and neutralises acidic metabolic waste excreted through sweat. Three baths per week — ideally evening before the magnesium glycinate.

🛁 The Full Mineral Bath
01
Fill bath with comfortably warm water — not too hot
02
Add 2 cups Epsom salt (magnesium sulfate) — muscle recovery, transdermal magnesium
03
Add 1–2 cups baking soda — alkalising, softens skin, neutralises acidic sweat metabolites
04
Optional: add 10 drops lavender essential oil — deepens relaxation before sleep
05
Soak for 20–30 minutes. Do not rinse off immediately — let the minerals absorb.
06
After bath: take magnesium glycinate and go to bed within 1–2 hours
💰 No Bath? Use a Foot Bath
A large bowl with warm water + 1 cup Epsom salt + ½ cup magnesium chloride flakes + 1 tbsp baking soda. Soak feet for 20 minutes. The soles of the feet are highly permeable. Sun-charged water adds an additional energetic dimension. This costs almost nothing and achieves a significant fraction of the full bath benefit.
⚠️ No Bath or Shower Filter?
Chlorine is absorbed through skin during hot showers and baths — and chlorine competes with iodine. If you're doing the iodine protocol seriously, a shower filter removing chlorine is the single most impactful environmental intervention. The shower filter referenced throughout this site removes chlorine, chloramines, and some heavy metals. Investment: once, lasts 6 months.
