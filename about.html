The Story Behind the Guide

Someone Who Chose
the Patient

Not a doctor. Not a naturopath. Someone who spent years inside the pharmaceutical system — and walked away when she could no longer stay.

S
Sharona
Pharmaceutical QA Internal Auditor · Independent Researcher

"I am not a Doctor. This is precisely why you need to listen to me."

The Beginning

A Diagnosis That Changed Everything

This guide was written with a lot of love and passion for helping people heal.

It first came about when my best friend was diagnosed with Parkinson's disease at a relatively young age — early fifties. I felt like someone hit me in the gut. And I knew I had to try and do something.

I had watched her move through the medical system. The neurologist. The prescriptions. The management plan. The slow, measured progression toward a future nobody was asking questions about. Nobody was asking: why is this happening? Nobody was looking at the gut. Nobody was looking at the environment. Nobody was asking what had changed in her body in the decade before the diagnosis.

They were managing. Not investigating. Not healing.

"The system was never designed to heal you. I know this — because I worked inside it."

The Pharmaceutical Years

The Business of Sickness

I worked many years in the pharmaceutical industry, in Quality Assurance — responsible for all documentation in the factory. My department was the paper trail of the entire operation. Every document from every department passed through my team. Regulatory submissions. Clinical trial data. Internal safety reports. Manufacturing instructions, specifications, and much more.

I worked in the room where the documentation lived.

I always knew that the system is not designed to cure anything. It is about symptom management. Each drug causes more symptoms and conditions that need more drugs. The more we get sick, the more profit for them.

"There is no financial incentive in a cure. Think about that slowly."

A patient who heals completely is, from a purely commercial standpoint, a customer lost. A patient who is managed — whose symptoms are controlled but never resolved — is a customer for life. Every side effect from medication A becomes the justification for medication B. Medication B creates the conditions for medication C. And so the wheel turns. Profitably. Indefinitely.

This is not conspiracy. This is accounting.

I watched it happen. In the paperwork. In the approvals. In the language carefully chosen to satisfy regulators while revealing as little as possible about what was actually occurring inside human bodies.

I am not saying every doctor is corrupt. They don't know. Nobody teaches them. Most became doctors because they genuinely want to help. I am saying something more specific: the system was never designed to cure you.

2020

The Decision to Leave

In 2020, the world changed. I started researching — frantically. I gave it everything. I had to find out the truth. What I found shook my core.

I morally could not keep working in the industry despite a good salary, benefits, and a position I was valued in. I was very good at what I did. But I could not continue working for a system that is built to manage illness rather than resolve it.

So I walked away. Not for money. Not for a better opportunity. I walked away because staying meant being complicit in something I did not want to be a part of.

And then I started looking. Not in medical journals funded by the companies whose products they were evaluating. Not in news outlets whose largest advertisers were pharmaceutical corporations. Not in government health agencies staffed by former industry executives.

I looked in places they don't want you to look.

What I found over the next several years changed everything I thought I understood about the human body. About disease. About what is actually making us sick and why. And what I found was both darker and more hopeful than anything I expected.

"You found this because you are ready. Begin."

Written with love, urgency, and everything I know — by someone who watched the system from the inside and chose the patient.
