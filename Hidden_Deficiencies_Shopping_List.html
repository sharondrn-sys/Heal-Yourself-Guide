Deficiency 01 Iodine — The Halogen War

The World Health Organisation estimates 96% of people are iodine deficient. The reason is not dietary failure — it is the systematic displacement of iodine from the body by three halogen competitors: fluoride (in water and toothpaste), bromine (added to bread flour in 1970 when iodine was removed), and chlorine (in tap water). All three halogens sit in the same row of the periodic table as iodine and compete for the same receptor sites. Replenishing iodine requires not just supplementing it, but understanding that you are fighting a chemical war in your own body.

Iodine serves different tissues: iodide is absorbed by the thyroid; elemental iodine is preferentially taken up by breast, prostate, and ovarian tissue. Deficiency 02 Alkalinity — Baking Soda Protocol

The body maintains blood pH within a very narrow range — any deviation is life-threatening. But the cellular environment, the lymph, and the interstitial fluid can become chronically acidic without affecting blood pH. This cellular acidity creates a hostile internal environment: pathogens thrive, enzymes malfunction, oxygen delivery decreases, and inflammation becomes entrenched. Baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) is one of the oldest, cheapest, and most effective alkalising interventions available.

What to Buy
Pure Sodium Bicarbonate — Aluminium-Freefrom your pantry
½ tsp in 250ml warm water with ½ lemon · Morning on empty stomach · Or as directed in the protocol
Standard food-grade baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) from a supermarket is entirely appropriate. The critical requirement: aluminium-free. Standard baking powder contains aluminium compounds — do not use. Buy pure baking soda (sodium bicarbonate only, no other ingredients listed). Bob's Red Mill, Arm & Hammer Pure, and similar brands are all appropriate. This costs almost nothing. The budget note here is not a compromise — it is the genuine recommendation.
⚠️ Never take with meals — neutralises stomach acid needed for digestion and B12 absorption. Take 30 minutes before or 2 hours after food. Hypertension or kidney disease: consult a practitioner before therapeutic use — sodium bicarbonate adds sodium load. Maximum 3.5 tsp in 24 hours.

Deficiency 03 Copper — The Forgotten Essential

Copper deficiency has become epidemic — not from inadequate intake but from excessive zinc supplementation without copper balance, and from the widespread promotion of zinc as an immune supplement during and after COVID-19. Zinc and copper compete for absorption: zinc consistently wins. Every person who has taken 25–50mg zinc daily for months without copper supplementation is likely copper deficient. Copper is required for melanin production (grey hair before time), iron regulation, nerve myelination, and ceruloplasmin — the iron-managing protein whose loss is implicated in neurodegeneration.

Copper Supplementation Options
Copper — NOW Foods 3mg Veg Capsules
1–3mg daily · With food · Morning
The safe therapeutic range for copper supplementation is 1–3mg daily — the amount that corrects deficiency without risking accumulation. NOW Foods copper provides the clinically relevant dose in a clean veg capsule. The correct zinc-to-copper ratio is 8:1 to 15:1. If taking zinc supplements, ensure copper is included. Symptoms of copper deficiency: premature grey hair, fatigue, immune dysfunction, anaemia unresponsive to iron, neurological symptoms, peripheral neuropathy.
⚠️ Do not take copper within 2 hours of zinc — they compete for absorption. Take at separate meals. Do not exceed 10mg/day. Wilson's disease contraindication: copper accumulates pathologically in Wilson's disease — do not supplement without testing if you have liver disease of unknown cause.

Deficiency 04 Zinc — Immune, Neurological & Taste

Zinc is required for over 300 enzymatic processes. Loss of taste or smell is one of the most reliable clinical indicators of zinc deficiency — a diagnostic that requires no blood test. Zinc deficiency also manifests as slow wound healing, recurrent infections, white spots on fingernails, delayed sexual maturation, and in severe cases, hair loss and dermatitis. The critical rule: always balance zinc with copper.

Zinc Supplementation — With Copper Balance

Bisglycinate is the most absorbable and gentle form of zinc — chelated with glycine for stomach tolerance and superior uptake compared to zinc sulfate or oxide. Do not exceed 40mg daily from all sources including dietary zinc. If taking 25–30mg daily, ensure 2–3mg copper is included in the daily protocol to maintain the correct ratio. Deficiency 05 Magnesium — The Master Mineral

Magnesium is required as a cofactor for over 300 enzymatic reactions — more than any other mineral. It is involved in DNA synthesis, protein synthesis, nerve transmission, muscle contraction, blood pressure regulation, and glucose metabolism. Approximately 80% of adults are deficient by current estimates. The standard blood serum test misses magnesium deficiency entirely — only 1% of the body's magnesium is in the blood; the rest is in cells and bone. The RBC (red blood cell) magnesium test is the correct diagnostic. Different forms of magnesium serve different purposes — form selection is important.
