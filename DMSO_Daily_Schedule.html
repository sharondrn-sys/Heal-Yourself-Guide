DMSO Protocol
Day-by-Day Application Schedule

A simple "start low, go slow" progression — patch test first, then build from 25% to 70% over three weeks while your skin and system adjust. Print the checklist at the bottom and follow it daily.

00 Before You Start — Every Single Session

These steps apply to every application, on every day, regardless of which week you're in. DMSO carries whatever is on your skin into your bloodstream — skipping this checklist is the most common cause of irritation and unwanted side effects.

Pre-Application Checklist
  • Wash hands and the application site thoroughly with unscented natural soap. Rinse fully and let the skin dry completely before applying anything.
  • Remove all synthetic clothing (nylon, polyester, spandex, acrylic) from the area. Wear cotton or linen only — and don't put synthetic fabric back over the site for 30–60 minutes after application.
  • Remove nail polish or synthetic cosmetics if working near the hands or face.
  • No lotion, sunscreen, or fragrance on or near the application site — DMSO will carry these ingredients straight into your tissue.
Application Method
  • Use cotton balls or cotton pads only — never synthetic foam applicators. If you need gloves, use natural latex only.
  • Apply with gentle pressure. DMSO absorbs within seconds to minutes.
  • Wait 20–30 minutes before covering the area or dressing.
  • A garlic-like taste or odour on your breath within minutes is normal — it means the compound is being absorbed and metabolised, not a sign of toxicity.
Dilution Basics

Always dilute with distilled water — never tap water, which carries chlorine, fluoride, and minerals that DMSO will pull into your skin. Pure aloe vera gel (100%, no additives) can replace water and tends to reduce irritation. Mix and store DMSO only in glass or polypropylene (PP) containers — it dissolves regular plastic.

01 Week 1 — Patch Test & First Applications

The goal this week is simple: confirm your skin tolerates DMSO, and let your body get used to the garlic odour and warming sensation before moving to a stronger concentration.

Day 1 — Patch Test
25%
Morning Apply a small amount of 25% DMSO to the inner forearm — a 2–3cm area is enough. Follow the pre-application checklist above.
All Day Observe for 24 hours. Mild warmth, redness, or a warming sensation at the site is normal. Itching that doesn't settle, burning, hives, or spreading redness means stop and do not proceed.
Days 2–7 — First Applications
25%
Daily If the patch test was clear, apply 25% DMSO once daily to your target area (see Application by Condition on the full protocol page for site-specific guidance). Always follow the pre-application checklist.
Note Face, neck, scalp, and other thin-skinned or sensitive areas stay at 10% regardless of how the rest of your schedule progresses.

02 Week 2 — Moving to 50%

If Week 1 went well — no excessive irritation beyond mild warmth or redness — increase to the most commonly recommended working concentration.

Days 8–14
50%
Daily Apply 50% DMSO once or twice daily to your target area, depending on the severity of what you're addressing. This is the concentration most protocols (joint pain, inflammation, wound healing) are built around.
Monitor Continue watching the skin reaction. A stronger warming sensation than Week 1 is expected. If irritation increases significantly, drop back to 25% for a few more days before trying 50% again.
Sensitive areas Face, neck, scalp, and spine stay at 10–25% — do not increase these areas to 50% or above.

03 Week 3+ — 70% As Tolerated

For musculoskeletal pain, nerve pain, and most athletic or injury applications, 70% is the traditional "standard" DMSO concentration. Move here only once 50% is well-tolerated.

Day 15 Onward — Maintenance Concentration
70%
Daily Apply 70% DMSO 2–3× daily to the affected joint, muscle, or nerve area for maximum penetration. This becomes your ongoing maintenance concentration for most musculoskeletal and nerve-pain applications.
Expect A strong skin-warming sensation is normal at this strength. The garlic odour will be more noticeable.
Sensitive areas Face, neck, scalp, and spine remain at 10–25% indefinitely — never apply 70% to these areas.

There's No Need to Rush

This three-week progression is a guideline, not a deadline. If your skin needs longer at 25% or 50% before moving up, stay there — there is no benefit to forcing a higher concentration before your skin is ready. Many people find 50% is sufficient for their needs and never move to 70% at all.

04 MSM Oral Pairing — Daily Foundation

MSM (methylsulfonylmethane) is metabolically related to DMSO and shares its sulfur chemistry. Taking it orally as a daily foundation complements your topical DMSO routine.

Every Day, From Day 1
Dose 1–3g of MSM daily, taken with food.
Timing Split into two doses — e.g. half with breakfast, half with dinner — to keep blood levels steady throughout the day.
Duration MSM is a long-term foundation, not a short course. Continue it alongside your topical DMSO routine for as long as you're using the protocol.
⚠️ Quick Safety Recap

Never combine DMSO with: pharmaceutical drugs (unless under direct medical supervision), hydrogen peroxide applied directly to skin, cisplatin or other platinum-based chemotherapy, or anything you wouldn't be comfortable injecting.

Do not use DMSO if you are: pregnant, living with liver or kidney disease, managing Parkinson's disease, undergoing active platinum-based chemotherapy, or have a severe sulfur sensitivity.

This is a summary only — see the full Safety Rules section on the main protocol page for the complete picture and sourcing.
