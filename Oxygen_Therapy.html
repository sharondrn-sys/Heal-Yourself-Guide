The Foundation

Otto Warburg and the Oxygen Discovery That Changed Everything

In 1931, German biochemist Otto Warburg received the Nobel Prize in Physiology and Medicine for his discovery that cancer cells use fermentation — not aerobic (oxygen-based) respiration — as their primary energy source. Normal, healthy cells burn oxygen for energy. Cancer cells, when deprived of sufficient oxygen, switch to a primitive fermentation pathway. This is now called the Warburg Effect, and it is one of the most studied and replicated findings in cancer biology.

"The prime cause of cancer is the replacement of the respiration of oxygen in normal body cells by a fermentation of sugar. All normal body cells meet their energy needs by respiration of oxygen; cancer cells meet their energy needs in great part by fermentation."
— Otto Warburg, Nobel Prize in Physiology, Science journal 1956

Warburg went further. His decades of subsequent research suggested that cellular oxygen deprivation is not just a consequence of cancer — it is a precondition for it. In his 1966 lecture at Lindau, he stated plainly: "Deprive a cell 35% of its oxygen for 48 hours and it may become cancerous." He argued that if the primary cause of cancer is insufficient cellular oxygen, then systematically increasing cellular oxygenation should logically reverse the conditions that permit cancer to develop.

This insight extends beyond cancer. Virtually all pathogenic microorganisms — viruses, harmful bacteria, fungi, parasites — are anaerobic to varying degrees. They are adapted to survive and multiply in low-oxygen environments. A body that maintains high tissue oxygen levels creates a cellular terrain that is systematically hostile to disease. Louis Pasteur, on his deathbed, is reported to have recanted his own germ theory: "The microbe is nothing. The terrain is everything." The terrain Pasteur meant is the internal environment of the body — and at its core, that means oxygenation.

This is not a fringe idea. The FDA itself approved hyperbaric oxygen therapy for multiple indications. Modern oncology uses oxygen-activating mechanisms in both radiation and certain chemotherapy drugs. Interferon drugs prescribed for multiple sclerosis work in part by raising systemic oxygen levels. The pharmaceutical industry builds expensive therapies on oxygen chemistry — it simply prefers that the public not access the direct version for 1.5 cents a day.

How Oxygen Deficiency Happens

  • Air pollution — urban air can contain as little as 10% oxygen vs the 20% ideal. Shallow breathing in polluted air compounds this.
  • Processed and overcooked food — cooking and industrial processing destroys the oxygen content of fresh foods. Raw, living foods carry dissolved oxygen; shelf-stable processed foods carry none.
  • Sedentary lifestyle — the body's ability to utilise oxygen depends on cardiovascular capacity and mitochondrial density. Both decline sharply with inactivity.
  • Antibiotic overuse — destroys aerobic gut bacteria that contribute to systemic oxygen production pathways and gut-based oxygenation.
  • Chlorinated tap water — chlorine strips dissolved oxygen from water and disrupts gut microbiome balance.
  • Chronic stress — sustained sympathetic nervous system activation causes cells to enter a self-protective closed state, reducing their ability to absorb and utilise oxygen even when it is present. This is the most underrated mechanism — and the reason stress alone can sustain illness even in people doing everything else right.

Hydrogen Peroxide

H₂O₂ — What It Is, Why It Works, What the Body Does With It

Hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂) is not an exotic laboratory chemical. It is a naturally occurring molecule found in every living system on earth. Your own immune cells produce it. When granulocytes — the white blood cells that are your first line of defence against every invading pathogen — encounter a threat, they produce hydrogen peroxide to destroy it. This is called oxidative burst, and it is one of the most fundamental mechanisms of immune defence known to biology.

Mother's breast milk contains hydrogen peroxide — highest in colostrum, the first milk. This is thought to be one of the key reasons breastfed infants have significantly stronger immune systems. Rainwater contains trace H₂O₂, formed when UV light acts on atmospheric oxygen. The famous healing springs at Lourdes, France, and the Hunza valley in the Himalayas — both associated with exceptional health outcomes — have been found to have unusually high naturally-occurring hydrogen peroxide levels.

When food-grade hydrogen peroxide is taken internally in therapeutic dilutions, it does what the immune system does naturally, but at scale: it releases bioavailable oxygen into the bloodstream, creating an environment hostile to anaerobic pathogens while nourishing healthy aerobic cells. Normal cells have an enzyme coating (catalase) that protects them from oxidation. Anaerobic bacteria, viruses, and most pathogens lack this protection and are oxidised on contact.

⚠️ The Real Risk — From Peer-Reviewed Medical Literature
Concentrated hydrogen peroxide (35%) ingested incorrectly is documented in peer-reviewed emergency medicine literature as causing venous gas embolism, haemorrhagic gastritis, gastrointestinal bleeding, and death. A 2022 systematic review analysed 126 reported cases — 17 deaths occurred, 13 associated with high-concentration exposures. Most serious events were accidental ingestions of undiluted or insufficiently diluted product. Properly diluted therapeutic protocols — e.g. 3 drops of 35% in 8oz distilled water — bring the final concentration to approximately 0.1%, which is in a completely different risk category. The risk is real at concentration, and minimal at correct therapeutic dilution. Both truths must be held together. Do not let the risk of concentrated H₂O₂ make you dismiss the safety of diluted therapeutic use — and do not let enthusiasm for the therapy make you dismiss the need for exact dilution.

The Inhalation Method

Bill Munro's Method — Direct Delivery to the Lungs

Bill Munro of Waterford, Michigan, developed and documented a simple inhalation method using a nasal spray pump filled with 3% hydrogen peroxide solution. He used this method for over 13 years and reported using it during a simultaneous bout with melanoma and prostate cancer. His self-reported outcomes are anecdotal — but the method has been adopted by hundreds of thousands of people globally, with substantial positive testimony particularly for respiratory conditions, colds, flu, and sinusitis.

The inhalation method delivers H₂O₂ directly to the mucous membranes of the mouth, throat, and lungs — the primary interface where respiratory pathogens establish themselves. At 3% concentration, the H₂O₂ is absorbed through the lung tissue into the bloodstream, providing both local antimicrobial action and systemic oxygenation. It avoids the gastric reaction that oral ingestion sometimes causes.

How to Do It — Nasal Spray Method

  • Use only 3% food-grade H₂O₂ (dilute 35% food-grade: 1 part H₂O₂ to 11 parts distilled water). Do NOT use the 3% drugstore pharmaceutical grade — it contains synthetic stabilisers.
  • Fill a clean nasal spray pump with the 3% solution. Wash a purchased pump thoroughly before use, or purchase a clean empty spray bottle.
  • Open your mouth wide, direct the spray to the back of the throat, and pump 5–10 times while inhaling deeply to draw the mist into the lungs.
  • Frequency: Morning and night for maintenance. Every 4–6 hours at first sign of illness. Every 2 hours during active viral infection. Most users report viral symptoms resolving within 36–48 hours.
  • Nebuliser option: 3ml of 3% food-grade H₂O₂ in a standard nebuliser is a more thorough method, delivering a fine mist deeper into the airways. Used by practitioners for respiratory conditions including COPD and emphysema. Some add 1 drop of Lugol's iodine to the nebuliser solution for added antimicrobial effect.
Caution — Start Low
Some people find the 3% solution strong for direct inhalation and prefer to dilute further: 1 part 3% solution to 1 part distilled water (giving approximately 1.5%). Start with fewer pumps and work up. People with reactive airways or COPD should begin with the most dilute version and assess carefully. If throat tightening or significant coughing occurs, dilute further or discontinue.

Ozone Therapy

O₃ — The European Standard That America Ignores

Ozone (O₃) is a three-atom form of oxygen. The third oxygen atom is unstable and highly reactive — it oxidises pathogens, stimulates mitochondrial function, activates the antioxidant enzyme system, and creates a cascade of beneficial biochemical responses in blood and tissues. When ozone dissolves in the body, it releases the third atom, effectively flooding the area with reactive oxygen.

In Germany, Italy, Russia, Cuba, and Mexico, ozone therapy is mainstream medicine — not alternative. Hundreds of published studies confirm its effectiveness against a range of conditions. A 2025 PMC review describes ozone as a promising immunomodulatory adjunct for treating immunodeficiencies, with multiple randomised trials and mechanistic studies confirming its effects on cytokine regulation, macrophage function, and NK cell activity.

Forms of Ozone Therapy

Major Autohemotherapy (MAH) — Clinical Setting

Blood is drawn from the patient, ozonated in a glass container, then reinfused intravenously. The ozonated blood is a vivid red — visually demonstrating the oxygen saturation. As ozone-strengthened blood disperses, it confers virucidal properties to the remaining blood, destroying pathogens systemically. Standard clinical practice in Germany. Requires a practitioner trained in bio-oxidative therapy.

Rectal Insufflation — Most Accessible Home Option

Ozonated gas introduced rectally is rapidly absorbed through the intestinal mucosa into the portal circulation, providing near-systemic effects without IV access. Used widely in Europe and Latin America. Requires a medical-grade ozone generator. Lower cost than clinical MAH. Significant community of practitioners and patients self-administering with proper equipment and education.

Ozonated Water — Home Use, Low Barrier

Water ozonated with an ozone generator and consumed immediately. Ozone is not stable in water for long (degasses within minutes), so timing matters — drink within 5–10 minutes of ozonation. Used for gut infections, oral health, and general oxygenation. Inexpensive desktop ozone generators are widely available. The EPA recognises ozone as a water treatment agent that eliminates 99.99% of bacteria and viruses.

Ozonated Oils — Topical, No Equipment Needed

Ozone dissolved in olive, sunflower, or coconut oil is stable enough for topical use and available pre-made online. Used for skin infections, chronic wounds, fungal conditions, and inflammation. The most accessible entry point for ozone therapy — no generator, no equipment, no clinical setting required. Very low cost.

⚠️ Ozone Gas Must NEVER Be Inhaled Directly
Ozone is toxic to lung tissue when inhaled directly. It causes pulmonary oedema and bronchial damage. ALL therapeutic ozone applications are into blood (IV), into the colon (rectally), into the ear, or applied topically in oil — never breathed directly as gas. This is not controversial. Even the most experienced ozone practitioners in Europe agree on this point absolutely. Any source suggesting direct inhalation of ozone gas is wrong.

To find a practitioner trained in ozone therapy, contact the International Bio-Oxidative Medicine Foundation or search for practitioners in your area through the German Society for Ozone Therapy (Deutsche Gesellschaft für Ozon-Anwendungen in Biologie und Medizin) or equivalent in your country.

Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy

HBOT — The Mainstream Oxygen Therapy That Can't Be Suppressed

Hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT) is the most clinically established form of oxygen therapy — the one even conventional medicine cannot fully ignore. It involves breathing 100% pure oxygen inside a pressurised chamber at 1.5–3 atmospheres absolute. Under this pressure, oxygen dissolves directly into blood plasma — not just haemoglobin — saturating tissues including damaged areas that haemoglobin-bound oxygen cannot reach effectively.

A 2024 review in Frontiers in Neurology (Bin-Alamer et al.) confirmed that HBOT promotes neuroplasticity through mitochondrial biogenesis, enhanced ATP production, VEGF/ERK-mediated neurogenesis, reduced inflammation, and synaptogenesis. A 2025 meta-analysis of TBI patients showed statistically significant improvements in cognitive function, memory, attention, executive function, information processing speed, and motor skills compared to baseline.
