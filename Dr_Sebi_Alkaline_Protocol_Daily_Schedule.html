Before You Start This Schedule

Dr. Sebi was explicit: the herbs cannot undo what the food is doing. The daily schedule is only as effective as the diet it sits on top of. Three things must be in place before timing and rotation matter:

1
You are drinking 1 gallon of spring water daily

Not tap. Not filtered. Natural spring water — it carries the mineral content the diet is built around. This is the single most important thing in this protocol and the most commonly skipped. Without it the herbs cannot move through the lymphatic system effectively, detox pathways cannot clear, and mucus cannot be expelled. Start here before anything else.

2
The kitchen is stocked with approved foods

You cannot follow this schedule if the food isn't there. Before your first full alkaline day: remove the excluded items (wheat, dairy, meat, commercial sugar, corn, soy), and replace with the approved staples — wild rice, quinoa, spelt, walnuts, hemp seeds, avocados, sea moss gel, and whatever approved fruits and vegetables are available to you locally.

3
You have at minimum the foundational trio

Sea Moss + Bladderwrack + Burdock Root. These three together are Dr. Sebi's baseline — the foundation everything else builds on. If you only have one product, this is it. Everything else in this schedule can be phased in over the first few weeks.

On Pharmaceutical Medications
Dr. Sebi's sixth law: take herbal compounds one hour before pharmaceutical medications — not simultaneously. This schedule follows that rule. If you are on prescription medications, discuss any herb-drug interactions with your prescriber before beginning. Do not stop medications without medical supervision.
The Core Structure

The Full Daily Schedule

Every time is a guide, not a rule. The structure matters more than the exact clock. What must happen before bed: 1 gallon of spring water, sea moss gel, the foundational herbs, no food for 3 hours before sleep.

6:00 – 6:30 AM
On Waking
2–3 glasses of natural spring water — immediately on waking, before anything else including brushing teeth. The body is in a deep dehydration cycle after 7–8 hours without water. The kidneys have been filtering all night and are primed to flush. This is the most important water of the day.
Add the juice of half a key lime if available — alkalising and activates digestion gently.
6:30 – 7:00 AM
Morning Ritual
Sea moss smoothie — the foundational mineral dose
Blend: 1–2 tbsp sea moss gel · 1 cup spring water · 1 burro banana · 4–5 dates (pitted) · 1 tbsp hemp seeds · ½ tsp bladderwrack powder · optional: ½ tsp seamoss powder on top
This delivers iodine, iron, calcium, zinc, selenium, magnesium, and complete protein in one drink. Take sea moss + bladderwrack + burdock capsules alongside if using the combined formula instead of gel.
7:00 – 8:00 AM
Breakfast
Seeded fruit or grain breakfast — choose one:
Option A — Fruit plate: Papaya + mango + berries + figs. Eat fruit alone or first — fruit digests in 20–30 minutes and ferments when trapped behind slower-digesting food.
Option B — Amaranth porridge: ½ cup amaranth simmered in coconut milk (or spring water) 20 min, topped with dates, walnuts, hemp seeds, and agave. Warming and grounding — better in cold months.
Option C — Spelt toast: Toasted spelt bread + raw tahini + date syrup. Quick and satisfying.
9:00 – 10:00 AM
Mid-Morning
First herbal tea of the day + water
Choose based on your primary focus (see Herb Timing section below for condition-specific choices):
· General: Burdock root tea (simmer 1 tbsp dried root in 2 cups water 20 min, strain)
· Respiratory: Mullein leaf tea (steep 2 tsp in hot water 10 min)
· Immune: Elderberry tea or capsule
· Blood/skin: Sarsaparilla tincture — 20–40 drops in a glass of water
Continue spring water — aim for 2 more glasses by mid-morning.
12:30 – 1:30 PM
Lunch — Largest Meal
The main alkaline meal of the day
Lunch should be the largest meal — the body's digestive capacity peaks in the early afternoon. Dinner should always be smaller.

Option A — Grain bowl: Wild rice or quinoa base + avocado + cherry or plum tomatoes + dandelion greens or kale + olives + grapeseed oil + pure sea salt + fresh herbs (basil, oregano, thyme)
Option B — Spelt pasta: Cooked spelt penne + homemade sauce (cherry tomatoes + garlic + basil + grapeseed oil, simmered 20 min) + wild arugula on top
Option C — Nopales plate: Grilled cactus (nopales) + avocado + cherry tomatoes + hemp seeds + key lime + sea salt. Traditional Mexican alkaline meal — one of the most mineral-dense lunches on the list.
2:30 – 3:30 PM
Afternoon
Second herb dose + sun + water
· Second dose of condition-specific herb (see Herb Timing section)
· 20–30 minutes of direct sun exposure — vitamin D synthesis, circadian rhythm regulation, mood
· Light snack if hungry: seeded fruit, a handful of walnuts or Brazil nuts, or fresh coconut
· 2 more glasses of spring water — you should be at approximately ½ gallon by now
6:30 – 7:30 PM
Dinner — Light
Light evening meal — 3 hours before bed minimum
The body needs 3 hours to complete digestion before sleep. Eating late forces the body to digest during its repair window (10pm–2am) — one of the most important principles in the protocol.

Option A — Large green salad: Wild arugula + watercress + cucumber + avocado + hemp seeds + key lime dressing (lime juice + grapeseed oil + sea salt)
Option B — Vegetable soup: Onion + okra + chayote + cherry tomatoes + kale + sea salt + fresh herbs, simmered 25 min. Add wild rice or quinoa if more sustaining is needed.
Option C — Stuffed bell peppers: Bell peppers stuffed with quinoa + chickpeas + cherry tomatoes + herbs + drizzle of grapeseed oil, baked 25 min.
8:30 – 9:30 PM
Evening Wind-Down
Evening herbal tea + remaining water
· Chamomile tea or lemon grass tea — calming, supports digestion and sleep
· Finish remaining daily spring water — aim to complete the full gallon by 9pm so sleep is not disrupted by bathroom trips
· Optional: 1 tbsp sea moss gel stirred into warm water with agave if the morning smoothie was skipped
10:00 PM
Sleep
Be asleep by 10pm
Dr. Sebi consistently emphasised the hours 10pm–2am as the body's primary cellular repair window. The alkaline diet and herbs mobilise toxins and support repair — but the actual regeneration happens during deep sleep. Consistently going to bed after midnight significantly reduces the protocol's effectiveness, regardless of how perfect the diet is during the day.

Precision Matters

Herb Timing — When to Take What

Dr. Sebi's sixth law: herbs one hour before pharmaceutical medications. Beyond that, timing each herb to your body's natural cycles significantly increases effectiveness. These are not arbitrary — each timing recommendation follows the organ clock and the herb's mechanism.

MORNING — 6:30 AM
Sea Moss + Bladderwrack
In the morning smoothie — taken on an empty stomach for maximum mineral absorption. The body is in a fasted, receptive state. Sea moss polysaccharides are absorbed more efficiently before food. Do not skip this.
MID-MORNING — 9 AM
Burdock Root Tea
Between breakfast and lunch — burdock stimulates bile production (liver) and lymphatic movement. Best taken when digestion is active but the stomach is not overloaded. Simmer 1 tbsp dried root in 2 cups water 20 min. The bitter compounds are the medicine — don't sweeten heavily.
WITH LUNCH — 12:30 PM
Sarsaparilla Tincture
20–40 drops in water alongside the main meal. Sarsaparilla's saponins work in the gut — binding endotoxins before they are reabsorbed. Taking it with food ensures it is present as the meal is digested. If using for hormonal balance, consistency at the same time daily is critical.
AFTERNOON — 2:30 PM
Elderberry / Mullein
Elderberry capsule or tea for immune and respiratory support. Mullein tea if there is any active chest congestion, cough, or asthma. The afternoon is when respiratory inflammation tends to peak — mullein taken here is well timed. Do not combine mullein with elderberry at the same dose; alternate if using both.
BEFORE DINNER — 6 PM
Prodigiosa Tea
If using prodigiosa for blood sugar regulation — 1 cup tea 30 minutes before dinner. The hypoglycaemic effect works by modulating post-meal glucose response. Taking it before, not after, gives the active compounds time to act before food arrives. Monitor blood sugar if combining with diabetes medication.
EVENING — 8:30 PM
Chamomile / Lemon Grass + Damiana
Chamomile or lemon grass tea for sleep preparation — mild sedative, digestive, anti-anxiety. Damiana in the evening if using for nervous system repair or energy restoration (not stimulating at these doses — it has a mild relaxant effect at therapeutic doses). These are the safest herbs to take close to sleep.
SPECIALIST — MORNING
Kalawalla (if using)
Kalawalla for autoimmune conditions — best taken in the morning on an empty stomach or with the sea moss smoothie. The immunomodulatory effect of Polypodium leucotomos compounds is most active during the morning immune cycle. Effects are cumulative — allow 45–90 days of consistent use before assessing.
Preventing Tolerance

Weekly Herb Rotation

The body adapts to herbs taken continuously at the same dose. Dr. Sebi's approach was built around variety and rest cycles. This weekly rotation prevents tolerance, gives individual organs rest periods, and ensures the full spectrum of benefits across a month.

Week 1
Foundation
Core trio only: Sea Moss + Bladderwrack + Burdock Root daily. No other herbs. This is the establishment week — let the foundational mineral and lymphatic support settle in before adding complexity. Note any changes in energy, digestion, or elimination.
Week 2
Blood & Lymph
Core trio + Sarsaparilla + Yellow Dock. Blood and lymphatic purification phase. You may notice skin changes (temporary breakouts are a common detox response — the skin is an elimination organ), increased urination, or changes in bowel habits. All normal if mild.
Week 3
Immune & Respiratory
Core trio + Elderberry + Mullein (if respiratory conditions present). Immune activation and respiratory mucous membrane repair phase. If there are no respiratory issues, elderberry alone is sufficient. Mullein is specifically for bronchial conditions.
Week 4
Nervous System
Core trio + Damiana + Prodigiosa (if blood sugar is a concern). Nervous system and metabolic restoration phase. Damiana addresses adrenal fatigue, nervous depletion, and sexual vitality. This week, prioritise early sleep and reduce screen time in the evening — the nervous system needs inputs to match the herbs.
Month 2+
Personalised
Rotate based on what your body is telling you. After 4 weeks you will have a clear sense of which herbs produced the most noticeable response. Continue the core trio daily indefinitely. Add 1–2 additional herbs based on your primary health focus, rotating every 2–3 weeks. Kalawalla and Yellow Dock can be run on alternating weeks if both are relevant.
Targeted Adjustments

Daily Schedule Adjustments by Condition

The core daily schedule applies to everyone. These are the condition-specific additions and adjustments Dr. Sebi recommended for the most common reasons people come to this protocol.

Autoimmune Conditions (Lupus, MS, Psoriasis, Rheumatoid Arthritis)
Add Kalawalla (Polypodium leucotomos) in the morning with the sea moss smoothie. Allow 45–90 days for immunomodulatory effects. Increase sea vegetable intake — dulse, wakame, and nori daily. Strictly eliminate all animal protein — particularly important for autoimmune conditions as animal protein creates inflammatory cascades. Add Yellow Dock for liver support alongside Sarsaparilla.
Respiratory Conditions (Asthma, Bronchitis, COPD, Chronic Cough)
Mullein tea twice daily — morning and afternoon. Elderberry capsule daily for immune support. Strictly eliminate dairy (the most mucus-forming food category) and wheat. Add Bladderwrack emphasis in the sea moss smoothie — the fucoidan has documented anti-inflammatory effects in bronchial tissue. Avoid cold drinks — all water and tea should be room temperature or warm.
Diabetes & Blood Sugar Regulation
Prodigiosa tea 30 minutes before lunch and dinner. Sarsaparilla with lunch. Limit fruit to low-glycaemic approved options — berries, key lime, papaya. Increase bitter greens at every meal (dandelion, watercress, wild arugula) — bitters directly stimulate insulin-sensitive pathways. Space meals evenly — blood sugar instability worsens with erratic eating patterns. Monitor blood glucose closely if on medication, as herbal support may reduce medication needs over time.
Skin Conditions (Acne, Eczema, Psoriasis)
Sarsaparilla is the primary herb — take twice daily, with breakfast and lunch. Burdock root tea daily (liver-skin axis — the skin reflects liver function). Yellow Dock for iron support if pallor accompanies the skin condition. Increase spring water to 1.5 gallons during active flare-ups — the skin is an elimination organ and flushes toxins through the dermis. Initial increase in breakouts during the first 2 weeks is a normal detox response; do not discontinue.
Low Energy, Adrenal Fatigue, Sexual Vitality
Damiana twice daily — mid-morning and evening. Sarsaparilla for hormonal and blood support. Prioritise Brazil nuts daily — selenium is the adrenal mineral, and depletion is common in chronic fatigue. Ensure the sea moss smoothie contains hemp seeds and dates — the combination of complete protein and natural sugars sustains adrenal function without cortisol spikes. Be asleep by 10pm without exception — adrenal recovery happens exclusively during deep sleep cycles before midnight.
Law #2

The Water Protocol — 1 Gallon Daily

Dr. Sebi's second law is the most neglected. Most people drink 1–2 glasses of water a day and wonder why the protocol isn't working. The gallon is not optional. Here is how to actually achieve it without forcing yourself.

6:00 AM
2–3 glasses (500–750ml) on waking. This is the easiest water of the day — the body wants it. Do it before anything else.
7–9 AM
1–2 glasses (250–500ml) with or after the sea moss smoothie and breakfast. Sip slowly.
10 AM–12 PM
2 glasses (500ml) through the mid-morning. Keep a 1-litre glass jar on your desk or work surface — seeing it makes drinking it automatic.
12–3 PM
2 glasses (500ml) with and after lunch. The largest water window of the day — the digestive system uses water heavily for lunch processing.
3–6 PM
2 glasses (500ml) through the afternoon. Herbal teas count toward this total.
6–9 PM
Remaining balance — finish by 9pm. Do not drink large amounts right before sleep — it disrupts the sleep cycle and you will wake for the bathroom during the 10pm–2am repair window.
💰 If natural spring water is not accessible
Filtered water is a reasonable substitute if spring water is not available or affordable. Sun-charging filtered water (leaving a glass jar in direct sunlight for 4–6 hours) is a traditional practice that Sharona uses personally — it does not replace the mineral content of spring water but is better than tap. Avoid reverse osmosis water without remineralisation — it strips all minerals and is essentially dead water.
