The Molecule

What Is DMSO?

Dimethyl sulfoxide. A naturally occurring byproduct of wood pulp processing. Found in garlic, onion, asparagus, corn, tomatoes, tea, and coffee in small quantities. Your body produces it endogenously. It is not a foreign chemical. It is something your cells already know.

What makes it unique — what has made it simultaneously the most studied and most suppressed therapeutic compound of the 20th century — is one property that no other substance shares at the same scale.

"DMSO passes through biological membranes with extraordinary ease — carrying other molecules with it. It does not ask permission from the cell wall. It simply goes through."

This property — called the "carrier effect" — is the reason DMSO is used in pharmaceutical research worldwide to deliver drugs into tissues. It is also the reason it can deliver anti-inflammatory compounds, minerals, and therapeutic agents to depths no topical application otherwise reaches. And it is the reason purity is everything. Whatever is on your skin when DMSO touches it goes with it.

Full Spectrum of Documented Effects

Antioxidant. DMSO is a potent free-radical scavenger — particularly of hydroxyl radicals, the most destructive reactive oxygen species the body produces. It neutralizes them on contact. This is documented in oxidative stress research and is one reason it shows protective effects in stroke, heart attack, and neurodegenerative disease models.

Antimicrobial. DMSO disrupts bacterial biofilm — the protective matrix that shields bacteria, parasites, and candida from immune response and antimicrobial agents. When combined with anti-infective agents, it dramatically increases their penetration and effectiveness. This is why it appears in combination protocols with antiparasitic and antifungal compounds.

Membrane permeability enhancer. Every cell membrane in your body is a selective barrier. DMSO temporarily increases the permeability of these membranes — allowing substances that would normally be blocked to enter. In therapeutic use this means co-applied substances reach tissues at concentrations impossible through any other topical route.

Vasodilation. DMSO dilates blood vessels and improves microcirculation. In conditions of poor tissue perfusion — peripheral artery disease, diabetic neuropathy, Raynaud's syndrome — this improved blood flow directly addresses the root mechanism of damage.

Collagen dissolution. Scar tissue, adhesions, fibrosis — DMSO has documented ability to soften and reduce abnormal collagen deposits. This is one reason it is studied for conditions including bladder fibrosis (interstitial cystitis — the only FDA-approved DMSO use in the US), systemic sclerosis, and joint contracture.

Nerve regeneration. Multiple studies document DMSO's effect on peripheral nerve regeneration. The mechanism involves both its anti-inflammatory and carrier effects — reducing the inflammatory environment that prevents nerve healing while facilitating delivery of nerve-protective compounds.

Intracranial pressure reduction. Published research in neurosurgical and emergency medicine literature documents DMSO's ability to rapidly reduce intracranial pressure — more quickly than mannitol, the standard of care, in some studies. This finding has been largely ignored by mainstream medicine despite replication.

The Story You Weren't Told

Why You've Never Heard of This

DMSO was discovered by Stanley Jacob, MD, at the Oregon Health & Science University in 1963. Within two years it was being used in hospitals across the United States. The early results were extraordinary — pain relief within minutes, inflammation resolved in hours, conditions improving that had resisted every conventional treatment. By 1965, 100,000 patients had been treated. Major pharmaceutical companies were racing to study it.

Then the FDA halted all clinical trials.

The stated reason: lens changes observed in laboratory animals given enormous doses. The scientific community responded immediately — these lens changes were species-specific, occurred only at doses far beyond therapeutic range, and had not been observed in any human patient. The FDA did not reopen human trials for years. Dr. Jacob spent the next three decades fighting for his research.

"A substance that cannot be patented cannot generate the returns that justify pharmaceutical investment. DMSO is a natural compound. Anyone can make it. That is the whole story."

DMSO cannot be patented in its natural form. It is cheap to manufacture. It works. These three properties together make it commercially worthless to the pharmaceutical industry — and therefore dangerous to its interests. The cost of bringing a compound through FDA approval runs into hundreds of millions of dollars. No company invests hundreds of millions to approve something anyone can sell for five dollars a bottle.

Today, DMSO is approved and used medicinally in more than 40 countries. In the United States, it is FDA-approved for exactly one condition — interstitial cystitis — while being routinely used by veterinarians, athletic trainers, and practitioners in every specialty. The research has continued despite the suppression. More than 3,000 peer-reviewed papers exist. The compound works. The system simply has no mechanism to benefit from acknowledging it.

Stanley Jacob was nominated for the Nobel Prize in Medicine. He treated thousands of patients at Oregon Health & Science University — patients who came to him after exhausting every conventional option. He published continuously. He testified before Congress multiple times. He was never wrong about DMSO. The science was never refuted. The suppression was administrative, not scientific.

He treated a child with a severe head injury who had been declared unrecoverable. The child recovered. He treated arthritis, chronic pain, scleroderma, nerve damage. He documented everything. He fought the FDA for 30 years while continuing to treat patients under an approved exemption at his own hospital.

He died in 2015. His research stands. The 3,000 papers stand. The 40+ countries that approved DMSO stand. The FDA approval for interstitial cystitis stands — a quiet acknowledgment that the compound is safe and effective, confined to the narrowest possible approval to minimize commercial disruption.

Morton Walker's book DMSO: Nature's Healer — a thorough documentation of Jacob's research and the suppression — was published and banned and republished. It remains one of the most comprehensive lay accounts of how a proven therapeutic was systematically sidelined for commercial reasons.

Concentration Guide

Dilution & Concentration

DMSO comes in concentrations from 50% to 99.995%. The correct concentration depends entirely on where you are applying it and what you are trying to achieve. Too strong and you cause unnecessary skin irritation. Too weak and the carrier effect is insufficient. Start lower than you think you need. Move up only when confident.

⚗️ Dilution Note

Dilute DMSO with distilled water only. Tap water contains chlorine, fluoride, and pharmaceutical residues — and DMSO will carry all of them through your skin. Distilled water or glass-distilled water only. Never dilute in plastic containers — DMSO dissolves plastic and carries the plasticizers with it. Always use glass.

⛔ Never Do This

Never apply DMSO over synthetic fabrics, dyed materials, nail polish, chemical sunscreen, conventional cosmetics, or any product containing petroleum derivatives. The Smell — What You Need to Know

DMSO metabolizes to dimethyl sulfide in the body — the same sulfur compound that gives garlic its smell. Within 10–20 minutes of application you will notice a garlic-like taste and odor. This comes from your breath. It is not harmful. It is not negotiable. It means the compound is working. For sessions where this matters — schedule accordingly, or apply at night before bed.

Application Protocols

How to Apply DMSO

The application method determines everything — how much absorbs, how deep it reaches, how long the effect lasts. The protocols below are based on documented research and clinical experience. Begin with the lowest concentration listed. Assess for 48 hours before increasing strength or frequency.

Before Every Application — Non-Negotiable

1. Wash the application area thoroughly with plain soap and water. No lotions, no oils, nothing else on the skin. Let the area dry completely — at least 5 minutes air dry. 2. Wash your hands with soap only. No hand cream. No nail polish. 3. Apply with clean cotton ball, glass dropper, or your bare clean fingers. Natural cotton only. 4. Leave the area uncovered for at least 20 minutes — no clothing over the wet application. 5. Do not touch the application area and then touch your eyes, mouth, or another body surface.

Application 01
Joint & Musculoskeletal Pain
50–70% concentration

Apply 2–4 ml to the affected joint or muscle group with a natural cotton ball. Rub gently for 30 seconds. Do not cover. Allow to absorb fully. Repeat 2–3 times daily for acute pain, 1–2 times daily for chronic. Relief typically begins within 15–30 minutes.

Skin will redden and feel warm — this is normal vasodilatation. If itching is severe, reduce concentration to 50%.

Application 02
Nerve Pain & Neuropathy
50–60% concentration

Apply along the nerve pathway — following the line of pain. For peripheral neuropathy in feet and legs: apply from foot upward along the calf and shin. For carpal tunnel: wrist, forearm, elbow. 1–2 times daily. Results typically require 2–3 weeks of consistent application.

Combine with Magnesium oil protocol for enhanced nerve healing effect (see Combinations section).

Application 03
Scar Tissue & Fibrosis
40–60% concentration

Apply directly to the scar, surgical adhesion, or fibrotic tissue. Massage gently for 60–90 seconds to assist penetration. Once daily for established scars, twice daily for recent ones. Results for established fibrosis require consistent daily application for minimum 4–6 weeks.

For new scars — wait until wound is fully closed with no open skin.

Application 04
Sinusitis Protocol
35–40% concentration

Mix 1 drop of 99% DMSO in 2 ml distilled water (yields approx. 35% solution). Use a glass nasal dropper. 1–2 drops per nostril, once daily. Breathe gently through the nose for 2 minutes after application. Do not exceed this concentration for nasal use.

Will cause temporary burning — this is expected and passes within 2–3 minutes. Use with food-grade hydrogen peroxide combination protocol for enhanced antimicrobial effect.

Application 05
Scalp & Hair Protocol
40–50% concentration

Apply to clean dry scalp with a dropper or gloved fingertip (nitrile glove only, not latex or rubber). Massage gently in small sections. Leave 20 minutes minimum. Wash with natural shampoo (no sulfates or silicones). Use 2–3 times weekly combined with castor oil or rosemary oil for androgenic alopecia protocol.

Apply castor oil AFTER DMSO has absorbed completely — not together. Carrier effect means oil constituents will also penetrate deeply.

Application 06
Oral Protocol (Internal)
Very low concentration — 25–30%

Add no more than ½ teaspoon of 50% DMSO to 250ml distilled water (final approx. 1% solution). Drink with food. Some practitioners use higher concentrations (1 tsp in 250ml) — begin at the lowest dose and assess over one week. Reported use for systemic inflammation, cancer adjunct, and neurological conditions.

⚠️ Oral use should only be done under practitioner supervision. Do not combine oral DMSO with pharmaceutical medications without professional guidance.

Combination Protocols

DMSO as Carrier

The carrier effect is DMSO's most powerful property — and its most important safety consideration. When you apply DMSO with another substance, that substance is no longer a topical agent. It becomes a systemic delivery compound. This works both ways: it can deliver healing compounds to depths impossible by any other means, and it can deliver toxins to those same depths if you are not careful.

The combinations below are documented — used in clinical practice, referenced in research literature, or established in naturopathic protocols. Always ensure the companion substance is food-grade or pharmaceutical-grade before combining with DMSO.

50% DMSO · 8% Magnesium Chloride Solution (Magnesium Oil) — mix 1:1 ratio

The most widely used DMSO combination protocol. Magnesium chloride penetrates poorly through intact skin on its own — topical magnesium effectiveness is debated for this reason. With DMSO as carrier, magnesium reaches muscular and nerve tissue directly. Documented use for muscle cramps, restless legs, fibromyalgia, nerve pain, anxiety, and sleep. Apply to large muscle groups (legs, back, shoulders) or along nerve pathways. 1–2 times daily.

Make fresh before each use. Do not store as a pre-mixed solution. Use food-grade or pharmaceutical magnesium chloride flakes dissolved in distilled water.

DMSO + Curcumin
50–60% DMSO · Food-grade Curcumin powder — small amount dissolved in DMSO first, then diluted

Curcumin has extraordinary anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and documented anti-tumor properties — but its bioavailability when taken orally is famously poor. The liver removes most of it before it reaches target tissues. DMSO bypasses first-pass metabolism entirely. Documented uses: joint inflammation, neurological inflammation, skin conditions, and as cancer adjunct. Apply to area of concern for local effect; apply to back of neck and spine for systemic/neurological effect.

Curcumin will temporarily stain the skin yellow — this is harmless and fades. Use pure curcumin extract (95% curcuminoids) — not kitchen turmeric, which contains oils and compounds that should not be driven systemically via DMSO.

DMSO + Iodine
50% DMSO · Lugol's 5% Iodine Solution — add 2–3 drops Lugol's per ml of DMSO solution

Iodine delivered via DMSO reaches tissues at concentrations impossible by oral supplementation or standard topical application. Used for thyroid protocol (applied over thyroid area on neck), breast tissue fibrocystic conditions, and as antifungal application. The iodine will be visible on skin as orange-brown — this fades as it absorbs. Rate of fading is a rough indicator of iodine deficiency (fast fade = high need).

Use Lugol's iodine — not povidone iodine, not tincture of iodine. Start with 1 drop and assess before increasing. See Hidden Deficiencies guide for full iodine protocol context.

DMSO + MSM (Methylsulfonylmethane)
50% DMSO · MSM powder — dissolve MSM in distilled water first, then add DMSO to 50% final concentration

MSM is the oxidized form of DMSO — what DMSO becomes after it donates an oxygen atom. They work synergistically. Together they provide sulfur donation, anti-inflammatory action, and collagen support at a level neither achieves alone. Used for arthritis, joint pain, skin conditions, hair and nail health, and systemic sulfur supplementation. One of the safest DMSO combination protocols — both compounds are exceptionally well-tolerated.

Use pharmaceutical or food-grade OptiMSM. The garlic smell with this combination is notably reduced compared to plain DMSO.

DMSO + Hydrogen Peroxide (H2O2)
50% DMSO · 3% Food-grade Hydrogen Peroxide — mix equal parts for 1.5% H2O2 / 25% DMSO final solution

One of the most powerful antimicrobial combination protocols. Hydrogen peroxide generates oxygen on contact with tissue — creating an anaerobic environment hostile to most pathogens. DMSO carries it deep into infected or inflamed tissue. Documented use: sinusitis, chronic ear infections, gum disease, nail fungus, skin infections. Applied topically — never orally or internally. See Oxygen section for H2O2 therapy as a separate protocol.

⚠️ Use food-grade 3% H2O2 only — never industrial or pool grade. The combination is mild enough for most applications but will cause temporary foaming and whitening of skin — harmless and passes quickly.

DMSO + Castor Oil
Apply DMSO first at 50–60% — wait 20–30 minutes for full absorption — then apply cold-pressed castor oil

Do not mix these together. Apply sequentially. DMSO opens the skin pathways; castor oil's ricinoleic acid then penetrates to depth it normally cannot reach. Used for breast cysts, uterine fibroids, ovarian cysts, liver support (applied over liver area with heat), joint inflammation, and lymphatic congestion. The DMSO-primed castor oil pack is significantly more effective than castor oil alone.

Use hexane-free, cold-pressed, organic castor oil. Apply a warm compress or hot water bottle over the area for 45–60 minutes after castor oil application to enhance absorption.

You experience: severe burning beyond normal warmth · blistering · signs of allergic reaction · unusual heart rhythm or palpitations · significant worsening of any existing condition.
