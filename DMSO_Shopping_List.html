Step 01 Sourcing DMSO — What to Ask For

DMSO (Dimethyl Sulfoxide) is classified as an industrial solvent in most regulatory frameworks, which means mainstream supplement retailers including iHerb do not carry it. This is a regulatory issue, not a safety issue — DMSO has been FDA-approved since 1978 for one condition (interstitial cystitis) and is approved in over 40 countries for various uses. More than 3,000 peer-reviewed studies confirm its safety profile.

⚗️ What to Tell Your Supplier

Ask specifically for: "Dimethyl Sulfoxide 99.9% pharmaceutical grade, in a glass bottle."

Glass is essential — DMSO dissolves plasticisers from plastic containers and carries them through the skin. Never use DMSO stored in plastic. Amber glass is ideal for UV protection.

Step 05 Safety Checklist — Before First Use

DMSO is exceptionally safe when used correctly. It is dangerous only when used without understanding its unique property: it carries everything through the skin. This checklist covers the non-negotiables.

✓
Glass bottle only. Never apply DMSO from or near a plastic container. It dissolves plasticisers and carries them through the skin.
✓
Skin must be clean and dry. Wash the application area with unscented natural soap and dry completely. Any substance on the skin — lotion, fragrance, synthetic fabric — will be carried inward.
✓
Natural fibre clothing only. No synthetic fabric (polyester, nylon, rayon) during or after application. DMSO will carry synthetic chemicals from fabric through the skin. Cotton, linen, or wool only.
✓
Distilled water only for dilution. Tap water contains chlorine, chloramine, fluoride, and other compounds. DMSO will carry all of them. Distilled or reverse osmosis water only.
✓
Start at 50% concentration. Never apply 99% undiluted DMSO to skin. 50–60% is the working concentration for most topical applications. Beginners should start at 40% and observe skin response for 24 hours.
✓
The garlic breath is normal. DMSO metabolises to dimethyl sulphide, which is excreted through breath and sweat. This is not a side effect — it is confirmation the compound is working. It is temporary and reduces with regular use.
