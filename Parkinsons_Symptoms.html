What To Do When It Happens

Every symptom has a mechanism. Every mechanism has a natural intervention. This section is practical — what to do right now, not just what to take every day.

Symptom Night Tremor — Waking Shaking at 3am
What Is Actually Happening

Parkinson's tremor is a resting tremor — it lessens during sleep, but wakes people when dopamine medication wears off overnight (typically 3–5am). At this hour, dopamine levels are at their lowest. The body is also most acidic, most dehydrated, and most magnesium-depleted after a night of metabolism. All three of these amplify tremor severity. The shaking is the nervous system screaming for dopamine support it no longer has.

Immediate Actions — When She Wakes Shaking
1
Do not panic — warmth and stillness first
Anxiety amplifies tremor immediately through adrenaline release. Warm blankets, slow breathing, and conscious muscle relaxation reduce the tremor loop within minutes.
2
Mucuna Pruriens — emergency bedside dose
Keep 250–500mg capsules on the bedside table. Take immediately with a glass of water.
Natural levodopa takes 30–45 minutes to cross the blood-brain barrier — the sooner it is taken, the sooner the tremor begins to ease. This is the single most important bedside intervention.
3
Magnesium glycinate — bedside
200–400mg in water, immediately
Magnesium directly relaxes muscle tension, reduces nerve excitability, and supports GABAergic calming — it works within 20–30 minutes and directly reduces tremor amplitude.
4
4:8 breathing — immediately
Inhale for 4 counts, exhale slowly for 8 counts. Repeat 10 times.
Extended exhale activates the parasympathetic nervous system within seconds — directly opposing the sympathetic (fight-or-flight) state that amplifies tremor. This is free and works immediately.
5
Warm foot bath or warm water bottle on feet
Feet in warm water with Epsom salt · or warm water bottle under feet
Warmth draws blood and nervous system attention to the periphery, away from the tremor-generating brain circuits. Epsom salt transdermally delivers magnesium within minutes.
6
Gentle rhythmic music — turn it on
Calm, rhythmic music at low volume. Something familiar and loved.
Rhythm gives the nervous system an external beat to synchronise to — bypassing the disrupted internal timing circuits. Even during tremor, the auditory system can entrain the motor system.

Prevention — the nightly setup: Take magnesium glycinate 400mg before bed every night. Take a Mucuna Pruriens dose at bedtime if nocturnal tremor is frequent — this extends dopamine precursor availability into the early morning hours. Keep a glass of water, Mucuna capsules, and magnesium on the bedside table permanently.

Symptom Freezing — Feet Won't Move, Body Locked
What Is Actually Happening

Freezing of gait happens when the basal ganglia — the brain's internal movement-initiation system — loses the dopamine signal needed to start a movement. The legs know what to do. The signal never arrives. An external cue (sound, sight, touch) can bypass the damaged internal circuit entirely and restart movement immediately.

1
March to a beat — count out loud or clap
"One-two, one-two" out loud. Clap hands in rhythm. Or play music immediately.
Rhythmic auditory cueing activates an intact alternative motor pathway that bypasses the frozen basal ganglia circuit — this is documented in multiple clinical trials as the most effective immediate freezing intervention.
2
Step over an imaginary line or a real object
Place a strip of tape on common freeze spots. Visualise stepping over a laser. Look at a target ahead.
Visual cues provide the external trigger the internal system cannot generate — the brain responds to perceived obstacles differently than to open space, reactivating the movement programme.
3
Rock weight side to side first
Shift weight slowly left-right several times before attempting to step.
Weight shift activates the proprioceptive and vestibular systems, which can initiate motor output even when the basal ganglia pathway is blocked.
4
Wear a metronome watch or use a metronome app daily
Set to 10% above natural walking cadence. Walk to the beat every day.
Daily rhythmic auditory stimulation training improves stride length, reduces freezing frequency, and builds the alternative neural pathway — effects persist for months after training stops.
5
Stick tape lines on floors at home
Horizontal strips of coloured tape across doorways and narrow passages — common freeze trigger spots.
Pre-placed visual cues remove the cognitive load of generating a cue during freezing — the line is already there, the foot steps over it automatically.
Symptom Rigidity — Stiffness, Pain, Can't Turn in Bed
✓
Magnesium glycinate — highest priority
400–600mg daily — morning and night split
Magnesium is the body's natural muscle relaxant — it directly opposes calcium-driven muscle contraction. Deficiency is near-universal in Parkinson's and makes rigidity significantly worse.
✓
Warm Epsom salt bath — every evening if possible
2 cups Epsom salt + 1 cup baking soda in hot bath · 30 min
Transdermal magnesium absorption directly into muscles bypasses digestive absorption limits — the fastest way to get magnesium into tight, rigid tissue.
✓
Tai Chi or yoga — every morning
20–30 minutes · slow, deliberate movement through full range
Morning movement before rigidity sets in prevents the progressive locking that worsens through the day — use it or lose it is never more literally true than with Parkinson's rigidity.
✓
Silk or satin bedsheets
Replace cotton bedding with satin or silk fitted sheet and pillowcase
Rigid patients cannot turn in cotton sheets — the friction makes it impossible. Satin reduces friction to near zero, dramatically improving ability to reposition during the night independently.
✓
Turmeric + black pepper — daily in food
1 tsp turmeric + pinch of black pepper in cooking or golden milk
Curcumin reduces neuroinflammation that directly contributes to muscle rigidity — black pepper increases curcumin absorption by 2,000%.
Symptom Sleep Disturbance — Acting Out Dreams, Insomnia, REM Disorder
What Is Actually Happening

REM Sleep Behaviour Disorder — physically acting out dreams, shouting, thrashing — is one of the earliest signs of Parkinson's, sometimes appearing a decade before motor symptoms. The brain stem circuits that normally paralyse the body during dreams are damaged. Sleep fragmentation in Parkinson's is also caused by rigidity (can't turn over), pain, and the progressive loss of sleep-regulating brain structures. Poor sleep accelerates neurodegeneration — this is not a secondary concern.

✓
Magnesium glycinate — 400mg before bed, non-negotiable
Calms neural excitability, reduces REM behaviour disorder severity, and dramatically improves sleep quality — the single most evidence-backed natural sleep intervention for Parkinson's.
✓
No screens after sunset — strict
Blue light suppresses melatonin, and the Parkinson's brain already produces less melatonin than healthy brains — any additional suppression destroys the circadian rhythm needed for dopamine recovery during sleep.
✓
Satin sheets — immediately
Inability to turn over in bed is a primary cause of nocturnal awakening in Parkinson's — removing the friction removes the waking.
✓
Passionflower extract — before bed
250–500mg standardised extract · 30 min before sleep
Passionflower increases GABA activity — the calming neurotransmitter that opposes the excitatory state causing REM disruption — without pharmaceutical side effects.
✓
Morning sunlight — 10–20 min, every single morning
The circadian clock is set by morning light — it regulates melatonin production 14–16 hours later at night. Missing morning sunlight is the most common reason for poor sleep that people overlook.
✓
Partner or caregiver awareness — safety first
Remove sharp objects from bedside. Consider a bed rail. Sleep in same room.
REM sleep behaviour disorder creates real injury risk — physical safety preparation is essential before any other sleep intervention.
Symptom Constipation — Critical, Not Minor
Why This Is Urgent, Not Incidental

Alpha-synuclein begins accumulating in the enteric nervous system of the gut years before it appears in the brain. A constipated gut is a stagnant environment where alpha-synuclein aggregates, pathogenic bacteria thrive, and toxins recirculate into the bloodstream and toward the brain. Constipation in Parkinson's is not a comfort issue — it is a disease-progression issue. Daily elimination is mandatory.

✓
Magnesium citrate — every night
300–400mg before bed
Draws water into the colon, softens stool, and stimulates gentle peristaltic movement — safe for permanent daily use without dependency.
✓
Psyllium husk — twice daily
1 tbsp in a large glass of water · always with a full glass · never without water
Bulking fibre that physically moves contents through the colon and feeds the gut bacteria that produce butyrate — dual action on both gut motility and microbiome.
✓
Squatty Potty or foot stool under feet when toileting
Any step that raises the knees above hip level while sitting on the toilet
The squatting position straightens the anorectal angle and reduces straining to near zero — Parkinson's patients who strain can suffer dangerous vagal nerve responses.
✓
Castor oil pack over abdomen — 2× per week
Directly stimulates lymphatic drainage and gut motility through the abdominal wall — the most effective passive intervention for gut movement.
✓
Sauerkraut with every meal + prebiotic foods daily
Restores the specific gut bacteria depleted in Parkinson's that regulate gut motility — addressing the root of the problem, not just the symptom.
Symptom Depression & Anxiety — This Is Neurological, Not Emotional Weakness
What Is Actually Happening

Dopamine is not just a movement chemical — it is a motivation, reward, and mood chemical. As dopamine-producing neurons die, depression and anxiety are neurological consequences, not psychological ones. They are not a response to the diagnosis — they precede it. This must be treated as a biological deficiency, not a character issue.

✓
Mucuna Pruriens — the primary intervention
Natural levodopa restores dopamine precursor levels — addressing the actual neurochemical root of Parkinson's-related depression more directly than any antidepressant.
✓
Saffron extract
30mg standardised extract daily
Multiple clinical trials show saffron at 30mg equals pharmaceutical antidepressants in mild-to-moderate depression — without side effects. Likely mechanism involves serotonin and dopamine reuptake inhibition.
✓
Exercise — every day without exception
Exercise is the most powerful natural antidepressant that exists — it directly increases dopamine, serotonin, and BDNF simultaneously, and is the only intervention shown to create new neurons.
✓
Omega-3 (EPA specifically) — 2–3g daily
EPA fraction of omega-3 has the strongest antidepressant evidence — the brain requires DHA for structure and EPA for mood regulation.
✓
Social connection — non-negotiable
Isolation accelerates neurodegeneration measurably. Social engagement activates dopamine and oxytocin pathways. Being with loved ones is literally neuroprotective.
Symptom Drooling & Swallowing Difficulty
✓
Conscious swallowing practice — 10 min daily
Deliberate swallowing every 30 seconds · chin slightly down when swallowing
Parkinson's reduces automatic swallowing frequency — the problem is not excess saliva but reduced reflexive clearing. Conscious practice rebuilds the habit and reduces drooling immediately.
✓
Singing and humming — daily
Any singing, 10+ minutes daily — even just humming while walking
Singing exercises the same muscles involved in swallowing and directly trains the oro-pharyngeal coordination that breaks down in Parkinson's — it also stimulates the vagus nerve simultaneously.
✓
Speech and swallowing exercises — LSVT LOUD
LSVT LOUD programme · speech therapist referral · or YouTube-guided exercises
Lee Silverman Voice Treatment is the most evidence-backed speech and swallowing intervention for Parkinson's — clinically proven to improve voice, speech clarity, and swallowing function.
✓
Upright posture during all meals
Sit fully upright, never eat lying or reclined · take small bites · eat slowly
Aspiration risk is real in Parkinson's — food and liquid entering the airways causes pneumonia, which is a leading cause of death in advanced disease. This is a safety intervention.
Symptom Fatigue — Profound Exhaustion
✓
CoQ10 Ubiquinol — 400mg with breakfast
Mitochondrial dysfunction is a primary cause of Parkinson's fatigue — CoQ10 is the central cofactor for cellular energy production and is severely depleted in Parkinson's patients.
✓
NMN or NR — morning
NMN 500mg or NR 300mg, fasted, morning
NAD+ depletion is a second major driver of neurological fatigue — restoring it supports mitochondrial function, DNA repair, and neuronal resilience simultaneously.
✓
MCT oil or coconut oil — 1–2 tbsp daily in food
Provides ketones — an alternative brain fuel that bypasses impaired glucose metabolism in Parkinson's neurons — reducing the brain energy deficit that drives both fatigue and cognitive fog.
✓
Exercise — even when exhausted, 20 min minimum
Counter-intuitive but evidence-backed: exercise reduces Parkinson's fatigue over time by improving mitochondrial function, sleep quality, and dopamine pathway efficiency — rest does not.
✓
Iron and B12 check — blood test
Iron deficiency and B12 deficiency both mimic and amplify Parkinson's fatigue — they are common, completely treatable, and frequently missed.
