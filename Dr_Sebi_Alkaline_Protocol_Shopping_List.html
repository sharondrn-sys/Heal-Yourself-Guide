Soursop Leaf — Graviola

500mg · 2–3× daily with food · Read the full protocol warning before use
The leaf of the soursop (guanabana/graviola) tree has produced some of the most striking anti-tumour ethnobotanical research available. ⚠️ Soursop / Graviola — Do Not Use If You Have Parkinson's Disease or a Movement Disorder
Peer-reviewed research has linked regular consumption of Annona muricata (soursop/graviola) to an atypical, levodopa-unresponsive parkinsonian syndrome — first documented in a cluster on the Caribbean island of Guadeloupe. The compound responsible is annonacin, a mitochondrial complex I inhibitor: one fruit contains ~15mg, and chronic daily exposure at this level destroys dopaminergic neurons in animal models. Sources: Champy et al., J Neurochem 2004 (PMID 14675150); Lannuzel et al., Mov Disord 2008 (PMID 18816693). This guide exists primarily for people managing Parkinson's and movement disorders — do not add soursop, graviola, cherimoya, sugar apple, or any Annona supplement to your routine if this applies to you.
